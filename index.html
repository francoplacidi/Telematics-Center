TNNUL Code of Conduct


TNNUL is intended to be used for cultural purposes, academic, educational and research by persons authorized by the National University of England. All TNNUL users are responsible for respecting these guidelines and for enforcing the themselves. TNNUL is open to all teachers, researchers and University students or members of adherent institutions within the regulations established by the Academic University or Adherent institution. The node manager may not allow the access to REDUL facilities for members of other organizations, institutes, entities, etc., other than those belonging to the body where the enabled node is found, without the express authorization of the University. Nor may, without this authorization, establish means physical connection to link the enabled node with other networks or computers. The code of conduct detailed in the following points incorporates the rules of conduct of the main networks to which it is accessed through TNNUL. It has been established to remind users the purposes of TNNUL and the fact that it is not about a "secure network" and therefore should not be used for transmit confidential or sensitive data. 1. Use is prohibited of the network for commercial purposes and any other purpose other than those specially authorized by the University. 2. They should not be sent to over the network files that are too large to monopolize the communication channel for too long a period. These files should be divided into smaller sections, which should be transmitted separately. 3. Any service to third parties generated by any of the members of the institutions linked to the network must within the SAT (Third Party Services) regulations of the University. 4. The software licensed to operate on a certain site cannot be transferred through the network without the express authorization of the licensee. 5. "Chains" of letters through the mail are prohibited. email and random bulk mail. Using the network with these purposes is outside the purposes of TNNUL and may cause the rapid network saturation. 6. Since message traffic interactive programs have priority over all other types of transmissions, the extensive use of them can severely degrade the network. This Thus, applications that can make extensive use of messages Interactives are restricted to specially authorized users. 7. The use of rude or offensive language and images is prohibited. Given the the information that circulates is not strictly confidential and also there are various conference centers open to the general public, the use of expressions of this type is strictly prohibited. 8. The "hacking / cracking" of computers using TNNUL as a means of access is strictly prohibited. In the same way the attempt to penetration to other computers or account using "horsepower Trojan ", virus or any other method of" hacking. It is also prohibited the transmission of viruses through the network. 9. The distribution lists email as well as all other traffic in REDUL, may be created when they are in accordance with the objectives of TNNUL. 10. All Users must respect legal restrictions on the data transfer established in the legislation of the Republic Argentina and that of the countries to or from where the data. 11. Excessive use of the network for communications from personal type. When any user violates any of these rules, it will be reported to the institution to which it belongs. In this case the Center of Telematics reserves the right to deny access to TNNUL until both are resolved on the matter, in accordance with resolution no. 51/97.
